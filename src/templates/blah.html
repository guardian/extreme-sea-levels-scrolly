Here you can see the location of 22 tide gauges around Australia for which the IPCC has estimated the increase in extreme sea level events. These gauges are part of an important global dataset for monitoring sea level rise

Best case scenario, 2046-2065

Here, locations are coloured by how frequent extreme sea level events will become relative to what is now a one in 100 year event. For many locations in eastern Australia, these events will occur once a year by the middle of the century even under the best-case emissions scenario (RCP 2.6). In other locations, the frequency has increased to once every ten years

Worst case scenario, 2046-2065

Under the highest emissions scenario modelled (RCP 8.5), most Australian sites will experience extreme events once a year from 2046 to 2065 and Brisbane is projected to experience extreme sea level events at least ten times a year.

Worst case scenario, 2081-2100

By the end of the century extreme sea level events become even more frequent under the high emissions scenario, with most locations projected to experience them at least ten times a year.
